High security risk (level 4) in connection with the spread of the coronavirus (COVID-19). With continuing restrictions in the flight and travel as well as far-reaching restrictions in public life can be expected until further notice. Of non-essential travel is not advised. As precautions to prevent further spread of the coronavirus (COVID-19) of the international air traffic is set to and from Saudi Arabia since 15.03.2020 until further notice, all borders are closed for passenger traffic. With 21.06.2020 the previous curfews (except Mecca, where will continue to apply a curfew 20:00 to 06:00) were canceled or withdrawn all economic restrictions.
Private meetings over 50 people are still prohibited. There is mandatory mask in public places as well as in the interior of private cars.

 Travelers are advised to avoid large gatherings of people to follow the instructions of local security authorities sequence and strictly comply with the hygienic precautions. More information about the coronavirus can be found on the website of the Ministry of Social Affairs, Health, Care and Consumer Protection and the World Health Organization. This year's Hajj (28.07. To 06.08.2020) is limited only permitted for residents in Saudi Arabia Muslims. Travelers who especially during the time of Hajj visit the Arabian Peninsula should apply strict hygiene measures. These include hand hygiene and avoiding potentially contaminated food (not fully cooked meat, raw milk, in particular of camels), contact with animals (especially camels) and contact with people suffering from a disease of the upper respiratory tract. In addition, pilgrims passengers are to pay attention to proper hydration and protect yourself in these areas to prevent mosquito-borne diseases consistently against insect bites.
