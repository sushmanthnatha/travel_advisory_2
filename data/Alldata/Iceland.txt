High security risk (level 4) in connection with the spread of the coronavirus (COVID-19). With continuing restrictions in the flight and travel as well as far-reaching restrictions in public life can be expected until further notice. Of non-essential travel is not advised. There are no restrictions for travel to Iceland and no restrictions for tourists. Transit at the airport Keflavik is still possible. Since June 15, 2020 are for people entering the following rules: Before departure to Iceland a Vorregistrierungsformular be completed. Upon arrival in Iceland is possible:  2 week quarantine or  a paid COVID-19 test at Keflavik Airport. Children who were born in 2005 or later are exempt from testing and quarantine. The only exceptions are travelers who do not come from high-risk areas, which are currently only Greenland, the Faroe Islands'own Islands, Denmark, Norway, Finland and Germany. In addition, all entering the country will be asked to use the COVID-19 App Rakning C-19th Further information can be found here. There is a ban on meetings for groups over 50 people; Hotel / catering, shopping malls, hair salons, swimming pools, libraries, etc. have opened in strict compliance with social distancing rules again. The Health Directorate in Iceland updates the information on their website. More information about the coronavirus can be found on the website of the Ministry of Social Affairs, Health, Care and Consumer Protection and the World Health Organization.
